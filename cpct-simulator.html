Everything You Need to Know About MP CPCT 2026

The Madhya Pradesh Computer Proficiency Certification Test (CPCT) is the ultimate gateway to securing government clerical jobs, Data Entry Operator (DEO), and IT Assistant roles in the state. Conducted by MPSEDC, this exam assesses both your computer knowledge via MCQs and your practical typing speed in both Hindi and English.

Our official simulator replicates the exact Unrestricted Typing Mode used in the real exam. This allows you to practice utilizing the backspace key strategically to fix errors while maintaining a high Net Words Per Minute (NWPM) speed. Consistent practice on this platform ensures that you won't be caught off guard by the actual TCS iON interface.

Frequently Asked Questions (CPCT Typing)

1. What is the minimum passing typing speed for MP CPCT?

To qualify the typing section of the MP CPCT, candidates must achieve a minimum Net Speed of 30 Words Per Minute (WPM) in English and 20 Words Per Minute (WPM) in Hindi. Scoring exactly these marks yields a 50% qualifying score, though higher speeds earn better grades.

2. Which Hindi font and layout is used in the CPCT Exam?

The MP CPCT portal officially supports Unicode fonts. Candidates have the flexibility to choose between two popular keyboard layouts during the exam: Remington (Gail) or Mangal Inscript. Our simulator allows you to practice on both seamlessly.

3. Is the backspace key allowed in the CPCT typing test?

Yes. The CPCT utilizes an "Unrestricted" typing evaluation method. This means you are completely free to use the backspace and arrow keys to navigate and correct errors at any point before the 15-minute timer expires. However, excessive use of backspace will naturally lower your Gross WPM.

4. How long is the CPCT scorecard valid?

Once you successfully clear the exam, your official MP CPCT scorecard is valid for 7 Years from the date of the examination. It can be used to apply for any relevant Grade-3 or clerical state government post during this period.
