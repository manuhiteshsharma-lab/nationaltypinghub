Top 20 Kruti Dev 010 Alt Codes You Must Memorize for Exams

📅 Updated: April 2026 ✍️ By National Typing Hub Experts

If you are appearing for state-level typing exams like Rajasthan LDC, UP Police, or UPSSSC Junior Assistant, you will be typing on the Kruti Dev 010 font. One of the biggest challenges of this non-Unicode font is that certain complex Hindi characters cannot be typed using standard keyboard keys. Instead, you must use "Alt Codes."

Missing an Alt Code during an exam is considered a Full Mistake, drastically reducing your WPM and accuracy. Below is the ultimate cheat sheet of the 20 most frequently used Kruti Dev Alt Codes you must memorize before entering the exam hall.

💡 How to Type an Alt Code

To type these characters, press and hold the [Alt] key, type the 4-digit number on the Number Pad (on the right side of your keyboard), and then release the Alt key. Note: The numbers on the top row of your keyboard will not work for Alt codes.

How to Practice These Codes?

Reading the codes is not enough; your fingers need muscle memory. We recommend typing out the "Example Words" listed above 50 times each in a blank document until your left hand naturally jumps to the Alt key while your right hand flies across the number pad.
