MP CPCT Exam June 2026: Typing Test Syllabus, Passing Marks & 30-Day Strategy

📅 Updated: June 2026 ✍️ By National Typing Hub Experts

The Madhya Pradesh Computer Proficiency Certification Test (MP CPCT) is the golden gateway for securing clerical, Patwari, and Data Entry Operator jobs in the MP state government. With the upcoming 2026 exam cycles, thousands of students are preparing to clear the hurdle.

While the Computer Based Test (MCQ) is relatively straightforward, the dual Typing Test (Hindi and English) is where 60% of candidates fail. In this article, we will break down the exact font rules, the mathematical scoring system, and a 30-day strategy to guarantee your CPCT scorecard.

MP CPCT Part 1: Computer Proficiency MCQ Exam Pattern

The official MP CPCT certificate is awarded based on performance in two mandatory parts during the same session. Part 1 consists of a comprehensive Multiple Choice Question (MCQ) pattern explicitly mapped out across 75 questions to be answered within 75 minutes. There is no negative marking for incorrect choices.

The computer-based portion rigorously evaluates candidates on six distinct intellectual and technical domains:

  • Basic Knowledge of Computer Operations: Booting processes, file management, peripheral setups, shortcuts, and core system utilities.
  • Computer Hardware and Software Familiarity: Core architecture, RAM/ROM definitions, device drivers, storage technologies, and application classification.
  • General IT Skills Proficiency: Network topologies, client-server models, secure internet protocols, email management, and browser operations.
  • Reading Comprehension Skills: Direct passage-based analytical comprehension questions to test immediate interpretation power.
  • Mathematical and Reasoning Skills: Basic arithmetic sequences, logic grids, pattern recognition, and core problem-solving models.
  • General Awareness: Indian polity, basic historical milestones, current state affairs, and foundational technological breakthroughs.

To pass the Part 1 CBT module, candidates must score a minimum of 38 Marks out of 75 (50% Threshold). Failing to clear Part 1 automatically invalidates your overall scorecard, regardless of your practical execution typing speed.

MP CPCT Part 2: Typing Test Pattern & Score Percentiles

The typing test is divided into two 15-minute segments. You must achieve the minimum Net Words Per Minute (NWPM) to pass. However, CPCT awards "Scores" (50%, 60%, 100%) based on how fast you type. A higher score gives you a massive advantage in final job merit lists.

Which Hindi Font is Used in CPCT?

⚠️ Kruti Dev is NOT Allowed

If you have been practicing on Kruti Dev 010, stop immediately! MP CPCT strictly evaluates candidates on Unicode (Mangal Font).

During the exam, before the Hindi typing test begins, the software will ask you to choose your preferred keyboard layout. You will have two options:

  • Remington (Gail): If you already know Kruti Dev, choose this! The key placement is 95% identical to Kruti Dev, making the transition very easy.
  • Inscript: This is the official Indian Government standard layout. If you are learning Hindi typing from scratch today, learn Inscript. It is more logical and does not require memorizing complex Alt-codes.

Official MAP_IT Software Scoring Evaluation Formulas

CPCT uses an Unrestricted Type of typing technique. This means candidates are allowed to freely use the backspace key to correct errors. Your final score is calculated using the following official formulas:

The 30-Day Strategy for 2026 Success

If you have 30 days left, divide your practice into three phases:

  1. Days 1-10 (Accuracy Phase): Do not look at the timer. Your only goal is to hit 95%+ accuracy. Use "Strict Mode" (disable backspace) during practice to force your brain to slow down and press the right keys.
  2. Days 11-20 (Endurance Phase): CPCT typing tests are 15 minutes long. Most students practice for 2-5 minutes and get exhausted during the real exam. Give at least three full 15-minute mock tests daily to build finger stamina.
  3. Days 21-30 (Speed Phase): Now push your limits. Try to type slightly faster than your comfort zone. Aim to consistently hit 35 WPM in English and 25 WPM in Hindi to account for exam-hall nervousness.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

How is CPCT typing speed calculated? ▼

CPCT uses an Unrestricted Typing formula. Your Net Words per Minute (NWPM) is calculated by identifying words separated by a space, taking the total correct words typed, and dividing by the 15-minute time duration.

Is backspace allowed in the CPCT typing test? ▼

Yes, CPCT allows "unrestricted typing", meaning candidates can freely use the backspace key to correct/modify errors while typing before final submission.

Which Hindi layouts are allowed in CPCT? ▼

Candidates must type using Unicode (Mangal font). The allowed keyboard layouts are Remington (Gail) and Inscript. Legacy fonts like Kruti Dev 010 are NOT allowed.

What is the validity of the CPCT Score Card? ▼

As per the updated rule book, the CPCT score card is valid for 7 years from the date of the examination.
