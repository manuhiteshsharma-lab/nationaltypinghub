Chapter 1: Know Your Layout

Mangal Inscript Overview

The Inscript layout is the standard Unicode keyboard designed by the Government of India. It is highly logical and is the mandatory format for exams like SSC CGL, CPCT (MP), and modern central government roles. In Inscript, vowels (matras) are generally typed with the left hand, and consonants with the right hand.

Chapter 2: The Core Rule

The Shift Key Logic

Inscript is beautifully designed. Pressing a key normally gives you the base consonant or short vowel. Pressing SHIFT + the same key gives you the related Aspirated Consonant (like क becomes ख) or Long Vowel (like ि becomes ी).

Chapter 3: The Home Row

Mastering the Middle Row

Place your fingers on ASDF JKL;. Here is what the Home Row produces in Mangal Inscript:

Left Hand (Matras)

  • Pinky (A): ो (O)
  • Ring (S): े (E)
  • Middle (D): ् (Halant)
  • Index (F): ि (Short i)
  • Index Reach (G): ु (Short u)

Right Hand (Consonants)

  • Index (J): र (Ra)
  • Index Reach (H): प (Pa)
  • Middle (K): क (Ka)
  • Ring (L): त (Ta)
  • Pinky (;): च (Cha)

Unlike Inscript, there is no strict logic; you must build raw muscle memory for every character.

Chapter 2: The Alt Code Rule

Understanding Alt Codes

Because Kruti Dev is a legacy font, some complex characters require you to hold down the ALT key and type a specific 4-digit number on your Numpad. For example, Alt + 0216 produces क्र. You must memorize these for the exam.

Chapter 3: The Home Row

Mastering the Middle Row

Place your fingers on ASDF JKL;. Here is what the Home Row produces in Kruti Dev:

Left Hand

  • Pinky (A): ं (Bindi)
  • Ring (S): े (E matra)
  • Middle (D): क (Ka)
  • Index (F): ि (Short i)
  • Index Reach (G): ह (Ha)

Right Hand

  • Index (J): र (Ra)
  • Index Reach (H): ी (Long i)
  • Middle (K): ा (Aa matra)
  • Ring (L): स (Sa)
  • Pinky (;): य (Ya)
