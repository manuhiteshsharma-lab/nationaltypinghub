Free Online English Typing Tutor: Master Touch Typing

Welcome to your ultimate online typing tutor. To clear Indian government typing exams like SSC CGL, CHSL, and Beltron, you must learn to type fast without looking at the keys. This technique is called Touch Typing. Follow our step-by-step guide to build finger muscle memory and achieve 50+ WPM.

Chapter 1: The Setup

Posture & Wrist Placement

Before you touch a single key, you must set up your body. Bad posture leads to slow typing and fatigue. Good posture gives your fingers the freedom to fly across the keys.

🧍 Body & Screen

  • Sit up straight with your back supported and feet flat on the floor.
  • Keep your screen at eye level to avoid neck strain.
  • Keep your elbows bent at a comfortable 90-degree angle.

⚠️ The Wrist Rule

  • Crucial: Keep your wrists hovering slightly above the keyboard or desk.
  • Do not rest your wrists flat on the table. Resting them acts like an anchor, limiting your finger reach and slowing you down.

Visualizing the Keyboard

Notice how the colors mirror each other perfectly. The red keys belong exclusively to your Pinky Fingers, the orange to your Ring Fingers, and so on. This symmetry makes touch typing highly intuitive.

Chapter 3: The Rules of Movement

Rule 1: The Home Row Base

Notice how the Index Fingers rest squarely on the bumpy F and J keys.

The middle row of letters is your "Home Base." Before you type anything, your fingers must rest lightly on these exact keys:

Left Hand:

  • Pinky Finger on A
  • Ring Finger on S
  • Middle Finger on D
  • Index Finger on F (Feel the little bump)

Right Hand:

  • Index Finger on J (Feel the little bump)
  • Middle Finger on K
  • Ring Finger on L
  • Pinky Finger on ; (Semicolon)

Both thumbs should hover gently over the Spacebar.

Rule 2: Reaching & Returning

When you need to type a key on the top row or the bottom row, you reach up or down with the assigned finger, strike the key, and immediately return that finger to the Home Row.

Rule 3: The Opposite Shift Rule

To type a capital letter, you must use the Shift key. The rule is simple: Always use the Shift key on the opposite side of the keyboard.
