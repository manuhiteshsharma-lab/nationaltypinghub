UPSSSC Typing Test Guide: Fonts, Speed, and Accuracy Rules

🗓️ Published: May 15, 2026 ✍️ By National Typing Hub

Securing a post as a Junior Assistant or Clerk under the Uttar Pradesh Subordinate Services Selection Commission (UPSSSC) is highly competitive. After clearing the written examination, candidates must pass a rigorous qualifying typing test. This phase is notorious for disqualifying many candidates simply because they aren't familiar with the strict font choices, time limits, and net speed calculations.

In this guide, we break down exactly what to expect on exam day and how to prepare efficiently.

1. Typing Speed and Time Requirements

Unlike many other government exams that only require typing in one language, the UPSSSC Junior Assistant exam strictly requires candidates to pass both English and Hindi typing tests. Failing either one will result in disqualification.

Language Minimum Net Speed Test Duration
English 30 Words Per Minute (WPM) 5 Minutes
Hindi 25 Words Per Minute (WPM) 5 Minutes

2. Which Hindi Font Should You Learn?

When preparing for UPSSSC, the most common source of confusion is the Hindi typing font. UPSSSC provides candidates with a choice on the exam software. You can select either:

  • Kruti Dev 010: A legacy Remington-style font. This is highly popular among traditional typists and is the default taught in most typing institutes.
  • Mangal (Inscript Layout): The standard Unicode font. This is heavily favored by tech-savvy candidates and modern government software because it maps logically to Indian phonetic structures.

Expert Advice: If you are starting from scratch, we highly recommend learning the Mangal Inscript layout. It is universally supported and required by many other national exams (like SSC and Beltron). However, if you already have a typing speed of 25+ WPM in Kruti Dev, stick with it! Do not try to switch layouts just weeks before the exam.

3. Understanding Accuracy and Qualifying Criteria

The UPSSSC typing test is purely qualifying in nature. The marks you score here will not be added to your final merit list, but you must cross the threshold to remain in the competition.

Your typing speed is calculated using the Net WPM formula. This means errors matter. Every incorrectly typed word, skipped word, or extra word is deducted from your Gross words typed. For example, if you type 35 words per minute in English but make 6 errors, your Net WPM will drop below the 30 WPM threshold, causing you to fail.

Aim for an accuracy of 95% or higher during your practice sessions to ensure a safe buffer on exam day.

4. Important FAQs for UPSSSC Typing

Q: Which Hindi font is used in UPSSSC typing tests?

Candidates are given the choice between Kruti Dev 010 and Mangal font (Inscript layout). You can select your preferred font on the exam screen before the test begins.

Q: Is backspace allowed in the UPSSSC typing exam?

Yes, backspace is fully allowed in UPSSSC exams. However, using it too frequently will decrease your overall Gross WPM speed, so it should be used sparingly.

Q: What happens if I fail the Hindi typing test but pass the English one?

For UPSSSC Junior Assistant, passing BOTH Hindi (25 WPM) and English (30 WPM) tests is mandatory. Failing either one will result in disqualification from the selection process.

Q: How is the Net WPM calculated?

Net WPM is calculated by subtracting your error penalties from your Gross WPM. Standard rules dictate that each mistaken word deducts from your total correct characters.
